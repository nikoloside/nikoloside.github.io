# Yuhang Huang (nikoloside)

I am currently a doctoral student in the University of Tokyo.
In was a game engineer focusing on computer graphics animation at the DeNA.

My research interests include computer graphics, computer animation, deep learning, fracture simulation, world model, deep learning in 3d space, trajectory prediction, and neural rendering.
In recent years, I am focus on creating the framework of real-time and realistic procedural fracturing animation and cross-section cutting simulation with Fruit Objects.

### My story

Hi! My name is Niko, and I come from a background in game development, where I explored creating fracturing animation in Unity.
Currently, I’m focused on building fracture animation and cutting simulation systems using deep learning and physics simulation during my doctoral research.
It allows me to push the boundaries of simulating real-world physics synthesised by deep learning in digital environments.

This passion is deeply personal. 
Inspired by seeing my family navigate the challenges of senior care, I realized the importance of handling spatial awareness of AI and Robotics with privacy and security. 
Systems that better understand 3D environments can help ensure dignified care for the elderly, improving safety, human mobility, and overall well-being.

With a strong foundation in simulation systems and a vision for applying AI to real-world spaces, 
I am eager to collaborate on innovations where deep learning, physics, and human-centered applications converge.
